    Contact Management Procedures

    Overview

    This document provides comprehensive step-by-step procedures for managing customer site contacts within the Towne Park Data Product platform. These procedures ensure consistent contact management practices, data accuracy, effective communication, and compliance with business rules and privacy regulations.

    Getting Started

    System Access Requirements

    Required Permissions

    • Site Administrator Role: Full access to contact management functions
    • Territory Manager Role: Access to contacts within assigned territories
    • Regional Manager Role: Access to contacts within assigned regions
    • Billing Administrator Role: Access to billing contacts across territories

    System Login Process

    1. Navigate to Portal: Access the Towne Park Data Product portal
    2. Authentication: Enter username and password credentials
    3. Multi-Factor Authentication: Complete MFA verification if required
    4. Dashboard Access: Navigate to the Contact Management dashboard
    5. Territory Selection: Select appropriate territory or region if applicable

    Navigation Overview

    Main Contact Management Interface

    • Contact Search: Global search functionality for finding contacts
    • Contact List: Comprehensive list of all accessible contacts
    • Add Contact: Button to initiate new contact creation process
    • Bulk Actions: Tools for performing actions on multiple contacts
    • Reports: Access to contact management reports and analytics

    Contact Detail View

    • Contact Information: Complete contact details and communication preferences
    • Contact History: Log of all interactions and communications
    • Associated Sites: List of customer sites associated with the contact
    • Edit Functions: Tools for modifying contact information
    • Communication Tools: Direct access to email and phone communication

    Adding New Contacts

    Pre-Addition Verification

    Customer Authorization Check

    1. Verify Authority: Confirm the person has authority to add new contacts
    2. Check Existing Contacts: Ensure the contact doesn't already exist in the system
    3. Validate Information: Verify all contact information is accurate and complete
    4. Confirm Role: Determine the appropriate contact role and authority level
    5. Document Approval: Obtain and document customer approval for new contact

    Information Gathering Checklist

    • Full name (first, last, middle initial, preferred title)
    • Job title and department
    • Primary phone number with extension
    • Business email address
    • Mobile phone number (if applicable)
    • Mailing address (if different from site address)
    • Authority level and decision-making scope
    • Preferred communication method
    • Emergency contact designation (if applicable)
    • Language preference (if applicable)

    Contact Creation Process

    Step 1: Initiate Contact Creation

    1. Access Contact Management: Navigate to the Contact Management section
    2. Select Add Contact: Click the "Add New Contact" button
    3. Choose Contact Type: Select the appropriate contact category:
    4. Primary Contact
    5. Billing Contact
    6. Operations Contact
    7. Emergency Contact
    8. Other (specify)

    Step 2: Enter Basic Information

    1. Personal Information:
    2. Enter first name, last name, and preferred title
    3. Add middle initial if provided

    4. Select appropriate salutation (Mr., Ms., Dr., etc.)

    5. Professional Information:

    6. Enter job title and department
    7. Specify authority level and scope

    8. Add employee ID if available

    9. Contact Information:

    10. Enter primary phone number with area code and extension
    11. Add mobile phone number if provided
    12. Enter business email address (validate format)
    13. Add alternate email if provided

    Step 3: Set Contact Preferences

    1. Communication Preferences:
    2. Select preferred contact method (email, phone, mobile)
    3. Set preferred contact times and time zone
    4. Specify language preference if applicable

    5. Set notification preferences for different types of communications

    6. Authority and Responsibilities:

    7. Define decision-making authority scope
    8. Specify areas of responsibility
    9. Set approval limits if applicable
    10. Document special instructions or requirements

    Step 4: Site Association

    1. Select Associated Sites:
    2. Choose customer sites associated with this contact
    3. Specify the contact's role at each site
    4. Set site-specific preferences if applicable

    5. Document any site-specific authority limitations

    6. Territory Assignment:

    7. Verify territory assignment for associated sites
    8. Confirm contact's role within territory structure
    9. Set territory-specific communication preferences

    Step 5: Verification and Approval

    1. Information Review:
    2. Review all entered information for accuracy
    3. Verify required fields are completed
    4. Check for duplicate contacts in the system

    5. Validate email format and phone number format

    6. Customer Approval:

    7. Send verification email to the new contact
    8. Obtain confirmation from existing primary contact
    9. Document approval source and date

    10. Save approval documentation in contact record

    11. System Validation:

    12. Perform automated validation checks
    13. Resolve any validation errors or warnings
    14. Confirm contact creation in all integrated systems
    15. Generate contact ID and reference number

    Modifying Existing Contacts

    Information Update Process

    Step 1: Locate Contact

    1. Search Methods:
    2. Use global search with name, email, or phone number
    3. Browse by territory or customer site
    4. Filter by contact type or role

    5. Use advanced search with multiple criteria

    6. Contact Selection:

    7. Select the correct contact from search results
    8. Verify contact identity before making changes
    9. Check for any pending changes or locks
    10. Review contact history for recent modifications

    Step 2: Modify Contact Information

    1. Basic Information Changes:
    2. Update name, title, or personal information
    3. Modify job title or department
    4. Change authority level or scope

    5. Update employee ID or reference numbers

    6. Contact Information Updates:

    7. Modify phone numbers or extensions
    8. Update email addresses
    9. Change mailing addresses

    10. Add or remove alternate contact methods

    11. Preference Updates:

    12. Modify communication preferences
    13. Update preferred contact times
    14. Change notification settings
    15. Adjust language preferences

    Step 3: Verification Process

    1. Change Verification:
    2. Verify changes with the contact directly
    3. Confirm changes with customer management if required
    4. Document verification method and date

    5. Obtain approval for significant changes

    6. System Updates:

    7. Save changes to primary system
    8. Propagate changes to integrated systems
    9. Verify successful updates across all platforms
    10. Generate change confirmation notifications

    Role and Authority Changes

    Authority Level Modifications

    1. Authority Assessment:
    2. Review current authority level and scope
    3. Determine required new authority level
    4. Assess impact of authority changes

    5. Identify approval requirements

    6. Approval Process:

    7. Obtain customer management approval
    8. Document business justification for changes
    9. Verify new authority with appropriate departments

    10. Update authority documentation

    11. System Updates:

    12. Modify authority settings in all systems
    13. Update access permissions as needed
    14. Adjust approval workflows
    15. Test new authority settings

    Contact Role Changes

    1. Role Transition Planning:
    2. Identify new role requirements
    3. Plan transition timeline
    4. Coordinate with affected parties

    5. Prepare role-specific training if needed

    6. Role Update Process:

    7. Update contact role in system
    8. Modify associated permissions
    9. Adjust notification settings
    10. Update reporting relationships

    Contact Deactivation and Removal

    Deactivation Process

    Step 1: Deactivation Assessment

    1. Reason Documentation:
    2. Document reason for deactivation
    3. Verify deactivation authority
    4. Check for replacement contact requirements

    5. Assess impact on operations

    6. Notification Requirements:

    7. Notify affected Towne Park staff
    8. Inform customer management
    9. Update relevant stakeholders
    10. Document notification completion

    Step 2: Deactivation Execution

    1. System Deactivation:
    2. Mark contact as inactive in primary system
    3. Disable access permissions
    4. Update status in integrated systems

    5. Maintain historical records

    6. Communication Updates:

    7. Remove from active communication lists
    8. Update emergency contact lists
    9. Modify notification distributions
    10. Archive communication preferences

    Data Retention and Archival

    Historical Record Management

    1. Data Preservation:
    2. Archive complete contact history
    3. Maintain communication logs
    4. Preserve approval documentation

    5. Store relationship records

    6. Access Control:

    7. Restrict access to archived records
    8. Maintain audit trail of access
    9. Implement retention period controls
    10. Ensure compliance with data policies

    Communication Management

    Outbound Communication Procedures

    Email Communication

    1. Email Preparation:
    2. Select appropriate email template
    3. Customize message for recipient
    4. Include relevant attachments

    5. Set appropriate priority level

    6. Email Sending:

    7. Verify recipient email address
    8. Send email through system
    9. Log communication in contact record
    10. Set follow-up reminders if needed

    Phone Communication

    1. Call Preparation:
    2. Review contact preferences and history
    3. Prepare talking points or agenda
    4. Gather relevant documentation

    5. Check contact availability

    6. Call Execution:

    7. Place call using preferred number
    8. Document call details and outcomes
    9. Record any commitments or action items
    10. Schedule follow-up activities

    Communication Tracking

    Communication Logging

    1. Automatic Logging:
    2. System automatically logs email communications
    3. Phone system integration logs call details
    4. Meeting invitations tracked automatically

    5. Document sharing activities recorded

    6. Manual Logging:

    7. Log in-person meetings and conversations
    8. Record informal communications
    9. Document verbal commitments
    10. Note communication preferences changes

    Follow-up Management

    1. Reminder Setting:
    2. Set automatic follow-up reminders
    3. Schedule recurring communications
    4. Track response requirements

    5. Monitor communication effectiveness

    6. Escalation Procedures:

    7. Identify non-responsive contacts
    8. Implement escalation protocols
    9. Document escalation actions
    10. Resolve communication issues

    Quality Assurance Procedures

    Data Quality Monitoring

    Regular Verification Process

    1. Monthly Verification:
    2. Review contact information accuracy
    3. Verify email deliverability
    4. Test phone number validity

    5. Check contact responsiveness

    6. Quarterly Comprehensive Review:

    7. Complete contact information audit
    8. Verify authority levels and roles
    9. Review communication preferences
    10. Update contact relationships

    Error Detection and Correction

    1. Automated Validation:
    2. Run automated data quality checks
    3. Identify format errors and inconsistencies
    4. Flag incomplete or missing information

    5. Generate data quality reports

    6. Manual Review Process:

    7. Review flagged records manually
    8. Investigate data quality issues
    9. Correct identified errors
    10. Document correction actions

    Performance Monitoring

    Contact Management Metrics

    1. Response Rate Tracking:
    2. Monitor contact response rates
    3. Track communication effectiveness
    4. Identify non-responsive contacts

    5. Measure customer satisfaction

    6. Data Accuracy Metrics:

    7. Track contact information accuracy
    8. Monitor data completeness
    9. Measure verification success rates
    10. Report data quality trends

    Reporting and Analysis

    1. Regular Reports:
    2. Generate monthly contact management reports
    3. Provide quarterly performance analysis
    4. Create annual comprehensive reviews

    5. Distribute reports to stakeholders

    6. Trend Analysis:

    7. Analyze contact management trends
    8. Identify improvement opportunities
    9. Monitor system performance
    10. Recommend process enhancements

    Troubleshooting and Support

    Common Issues and Solutions

    Contact Information Issues

    1. Bounced Emails:
    2. Verify email address accuracy
    3. Check for typos or format errors
    4. Contact customer to confirm email

    5. Update email address if needed

    6. Invalid Phone Numbers:

    7. Verify phone number format
    8. Check area codes and extensions
    9. Test alternative contact methods
    10. Update phone information

    System Access Issues

    1. Permission Problems:
    2. Verify user role and permissions
    3. Check territory assignments
    4. Contact system administrator

    5. Request permission updates

    6. System Performance Issues:

    7. Clear browser cache and cookies
    8. Try alternative browser
    9. Check internet connection
    10. Report system issues to IT support

    Escalation Procedures

    Internal Escalation

    1. Level 1 - Supervisor:
    2. Contact immediate supervisor
    3. Provide issue details and context
    4. Request guidance or assistance

    5. Document escalation actions

    6. Level 2 - Management:

    7. Escalate to department management
    8. Provide comprehensive issue summary
    9. Include previous resolution attempts
    10. Request management intervention

    External Escalation

    1. Customer Escalation:
    2. Contact customer management
    3. Explain issue and impact
    4. Request customer assistance

    5. Document customer response

    6. Vendor Escalation:

    7. Contact system vendor support
    8. Provide technical issue details
    9. Request vendor assistance
    10. Track vendor response and resolution

    This comprehensive contact management procedures guide ensures consistent, effective, and compliant contact management practices within the Towne Park Data Product platform while maintaining high standards of customer service and data quality.
